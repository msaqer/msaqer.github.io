Trustworthy Language Intelligence Lab (TLI Lab)

Reliable, efficient, and auditable NLP for security-relevant and harmful online text.

The TLI Lab develops reproducible evaluation assets and practical NLP pipelines that support deployment-realistic decisions across traditional ML, transformers, and LLMs, with explicit attention to latency, cost, and energy (Green NLP).

Research Thrusts

1) Security-Aware Language Intelligence

Incident-centric discourse triage and prioritization for cybersecurity events—especially healthcare cyber incidents—using transparent scoring, filtering, and analysis workflows.

Example: CyberTweetGrader&Labeler (CTGL) for prioritizing cyber-incident tweets.

2) Adversarial Machine Learning, Robustness, and Trustworthy AI

Empirical study of how machine learning systems behave under adversarial and security-relevant conditions, including privacy leakage, training-time attacks, and inference-time robustness failures.

Examples: membership inference, data poisoning, and evasion attacks across classical ML and deep learning models.

3) Comparative Benchmarking: ML ↔ Transformers ↔ LLMs

Controlled, reproducible comparisons that quantify not only predictive performance, but also reliability, interpretability, and deployment trade-offs such as cost, latency, and failure modes.

Example: prompted LLM inference vs. fine-tuned encoders under realistic constraints.

4) Green NLP & Efficient Inference

Energy-aware evaluation that supports sustainable model selection—e.g., TF-IDF baselines vs. fine-tuned transformers—while maintaining responsible and reproducible reporting.

Example: accuracy–latency–energy trade-offs for harmful-language detection.

Current / Featured Projects

  • CyberTweetGrader&Labeler (CTGL): A domain-specific NLP system for prioritizing cyber-incident discourse on Twitter/X. Project page.
  • ML security and privacy baselines: Student-led, reproducible studies of evasion attacks, membership inference, and data poisoning accepted for MICS 2026.
  • Trust-calibrated evaluation of LLM inference vs. fine-tuned encoders: Controlled comparisons under reliability and cost constraints.
  • Green NLP for online abuse detection: Joint evaluation of accuracy, throughput/latency, and energy per inference for classical baselines vs. transformers.
  • Harmful language detection (cyberbullying / abuse / AI-generated text): Comparative benchmarking and error analysis across model families.
